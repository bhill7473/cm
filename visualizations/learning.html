Learning Outcomes

Overall, students in the Construction Management major learn how to understand the regulations, methods, and materials of the construction industry to uphold professional and ethical standards and maintain a safe work environment. Upon graduating, they can accurately interpret construction documents, construct computerized schedules, and provide accurate estimates for expenses and materials. Their education also prepares them to use critical thinking and strong communication to maneuver complex projects.

Program-Specific Requirements

In addition to the Kent Core requirements, Construction Management students take on a challenging curriculum and maintain a minimum major GPA of 2.250 and a minimum overall GPA of 2.000 to graduate. The diverse program of studies includes courses in construction safety, construction materials and methods, surveying, mechanical and electrical systems, scheduling, management, sustainability, accounting, economics, technical writing, business, marketing, calculus, trigonometry, and physics.
