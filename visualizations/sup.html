Supplementary Education

Students studying Construction Management at Kent State have a wide range of opportunities for augmenting the core curriculum both in and out of the classroom. For example, Construction Management students can choose one of three optional concentrations -- Civil Management, Mechanical and Electrical Management, and Safety Management -- which require enrollment in upper-level courses tailored to the type of management chosen. Additionally, students can work in conjunction with local construction companies and engineering firms to further their practical education through experiential learning opportunities. Finally, students may choose to pair their Construction Management degree with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Architectural Studies.

Program-Specific Requirements

In addition to the Kent Core requirements, Construction Management students take on a challenging curriculum and maintain a minimum major GPA of 2.250 and a minimum overall GPA of 2.000 to graduate. The diverse program of studies includes courses in construction safety, construction materials and methods, surveying, mechanical and electrical systems, scheduling, management, sustainability, accounting, economics, technical writing, business, marketing, calculus, trigonometry, and physics.
